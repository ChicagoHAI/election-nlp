Generate a comparative summary of Party A's policy platforms from two different years, highlighting which policies and positions have changed or remained the same.

# Steps

1. **Identify Issues:** Review the provided text for key issues mentioned by Party A in either year, such as the economy, foreign policy, immigration, social issues, education, etc.
2. **Extract Party Positions:** For each issue, extract and summarize the key points or positions of Party A from the text for each year.
3. **Compare and Contrast:** Identify and list policies and positions that have changed or remained the same for each issue where Party A provides information across both years.
4. **Organize by Issue:** Structure the summary issue by issue, providing a balanced and unbiased comparison.

# Output Format

For each issue, present the summary as follows:

**[Issue name]**
- [Year 1]: [1-2 sentence summary from the provided text]
- [Year 2]: [1-2 sentence summary from the provided text]
- What remained the same: [one sentence]
- What changed: [one sentence]

Specify "Not mentioned" if Party A does not address an issue in a particular year. 
If an issue is not mentioned in one year, exclude "what remained the same" and "what changed".

# Examples

**Foreign Policy**
- 2016: Focuses on strengthening military to fight against ISIS in Syria.
- 2024: Prioritizes diplomacy to bring peace to Ukraine and Israel/Palestine. 
- What remained the same: Party A emphasizes America's responsibility to preserve peace abroad.
- What changed: Party A changed focus to different regions, and their priorities shifted from military to diplomatic strength. 

**Education**
- 2016: Not mentioned
- 2024: Proposes increasing funding for public schools.


# Notes
- Only use information directly from the provided text. Avoid referencing any external information about the party.
- Ensure all comparisons are unbiased and clearly differentiate based on the party's current and past platforms.
- Make the summary accurate and concise.
- Your summary is very important. Many undecided voters will use this summary to decide who to vote for.
